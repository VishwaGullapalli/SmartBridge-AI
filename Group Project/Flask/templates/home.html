Prophet Model

This project report focuses on utilizing the Prophet model, a robust forecasting tool developed by Facebook's Core Data Science team, to predict commuters' inflow for the airline industry. The Prophet model has gained popularity for its ability to handle various time series data, incorporating trend, seasonality, and holiday effects.

The Prophet model is a popular time series forecasting model developed by Facebook's Core Data Science team. It is designed to provide accurate and efficient predictions for time series data, particularly those with strong seasonal patterns. Here's some information about the Prophet model and its uses:

What is the Prophet model?

The Prophet model is an open-source forecasting model developed by Facebook. It is based on an additive model that decomposes time series data into trend, seasonality, and holiday components. The model utilizes a Bayesian approach, allowing for the inclusion of prior knowledge and uncertainty estimation in the forecasts.

How does the Prophet model work?

The Prophet model decomposes time series data into trend and seasonality components. It also considers the impact of holidays and other important events. It fits a piecewise linear or logistic growth curve to the trend and uses Fourier series to model seasonality. The model is flexible and can handle missing data, outliers, and changes in the trend.

What are the key features and advantages of the Prophet model?

1. Automatic detection and handling of seasonality, including multiple seasonalities and daily, weekly, and yearly patterns.
2. Accommodation of custom seasonalities, such as irregular events or business-specific variations.
3. Robustness against missing data and outliers, as it can handle these issues effectively.
4. Ability to incorporate external factors, such as holidays and special events, to improve forecasting accuracy.
5. Transparency in model interpretation and easy implementation, even for users without extensive statistical background.

About the Data Set

Data set Link : Click here

The dataset is structured as a tabular form with two primary dimensions: time and passenger count. The time dimension represents the sequential order of the observations, spanning from January 1949 to December 1960. The passenger count dimension signifies the recorded number of passengers corresponding to each respective time period.

The dataset encompasses a total of 144 observations, signifying the duration of 12 years. Each observation captures the passenger count for a distinct month, resulting in a monthly temporal resolution for the data.

Overall, the dataset provides valuable insights into the historical passenger trends, reflecting the dynamic nature of air travel demand and the changing preferences of travelers over the studied period. Further analysis, visualization, and modeling of the dataset can uncover additional insights and facilitate more accurate forecasts and predictions for future passenger counts.
